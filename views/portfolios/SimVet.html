Simulation, Validation, Evaluation and Testing (SimVET)

SimVET-Vetting Healthcare Solutions

Simulation Validation, Evaluation and Testing (SimVET) is an emerging innovative model and service sponsored by the National Simulation Center (NSC) to test drive healthcare solutions (e.g., clinical software applications, medical devices) in a fail-safe simulated clinical environment prior to acquisition and deployment across the VHA and in VA Medical Centers. SimVET supports VHA modernization efforts to become a high reliability organization (HRO) by anticipating risks that could potentially lead to harmful events. This is achieved by empowering front-line staff and clinicians who directly provide care to Veterans to be an essential component of the vetting process.



By replicating complex care environments such as Intensive Care Units (ICU), Community-Based Outpatient Clinics (CBOC) and Community Living Centers (CLC), SimVET develops use-case scenarios to analyze healthcare solutions for usability, patient safety, impact on clinical workflow and technical feasibility.

Mission

To test drive health care solutions with frontline staff in a simulated hospital environment.

Vision

Nothing should touch a Veteran until it has been validated as safe and effective through simulation.

Why Simulation?



Veterans spent a career simulating and practicing some of the most dangerous and complex things that could happen, nearly every eventuality.



“Military service members go into harm’s way; our Veterans should not.”

What We Achieve

Simulating high-risk, complex real-life situations is nothing new to the military. Since World War II, pilots have conducted flight simulations in cockpits to prepare for deployment. In 1999, the Army used clinical simulation as a core element in training combat medics in pre-hospital trauma care to improve survivor outcomes. Today, immersive simulation such as that offered by the NSC, has been well researched as a method to enhance the knowledge, skills and attitudes of clinicians to deliver high value, quality care. The VHA and NSC are well positioned to expand the use of clinical simulation from its traditional utility (training clinicians to perform safe care to engaging clinicians in front-line innovation) to advance how care is delivered to Veterans.


  • A streamlined, reproducible process for validating, evaluating, and testing healthcare solutions for usability, patient safety, technical feasibility, and clinical workflow impact.
  • A simulation environment that accurately represents diverse clinical settings across medical centers and scalable across SimVET network sites. ​
  • A “Consumer Reports” style analysis for each healthcare solution that is validated, evaluated, and tested.
  • A repository of SimVET analysis results for the enterprise.
  • Decreased cost, variability, and interoperability challenges that lead to potential patient safety hazards, clinical workflow disruptions, and an inefficient use of resources across healthcare facilities.
