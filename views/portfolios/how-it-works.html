The Approach/How it Works

The SimVET model will analyze a variety of healthcare solutions (e.g., clinical software applications and medical equipment/devices) through examining usability, impact on clinical workflow, patient safety, and technical feasibility. The SimVET model will produce a “Consumer Reports” style analysis including pros and cons, relevant comparisons, unique advantages, patient safety risks, and end-user recommendations. This final report is intended to inform acquisition decisions throughout the enterprise. In short, SimVET allows the VHA to “test drive” healthcare solutions before they reach Veterans and clinical end-users. The criteria for healthcare solution analysis will be based on priorities from leadership in the VHA, VISN, or VAMC levels and/or recommendations from the VHA Program Offices or other stakeholders such as staff and Veterans. To ensure the solution can work in the VHA environment, technical requirements will be tested. Frontline clinical staff will conduct live scenarios in a realistic, simulated hospital environment, applying these new healthcare solutions in the workflows for patient care and other Veteran-facing services.

The Challenge

The VHA recognizes there are opportunities to create a more streamlined process for vetting healthcare solutions prior to acquisition. This can result in increased cost and variability in healthcare solutions deployed across Veterans Integrated Services Networks (VISNs). Additionally, this can create interoperability challenges that lead to potential patient safety hazards, clinical workflow disruptions, and an inefficient use of resources. The lack of a centralized and streamlined process creates limitations in supporting the VHA’s mission to provide world-class healthcare for Veterans. To address these issues, SimVET seeks to support acquisition decisions by using simulation to validate, evaluate and test healthcare solutions throughout the enterprise.
