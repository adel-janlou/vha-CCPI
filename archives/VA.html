History of Innovation @ VA

VA has long embraced innovation, from the first implantable cardiac pacemaker in 1960 to the recent exploration of clinical virtual reality (VR) applications and 3D bioprinting to fabricate patient-matched, vascularized, living bone. VA empowers frontline staff to take calculated risks, methodically test assumptions, learn from failure, and thrive in uncertainty. Our ability to adapt and rapidly spread innovation throughout the VA, shows us every day what it means to be a learning organization. This is one of our most critical resources in delivering the best care to Veterans. Today, VA is cultivating dynamic partnerships with federal agencies, nonprofits, and private industries, as well as collaborating with academic affiliates to test innovative solutions through research.
