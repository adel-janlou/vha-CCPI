Tackling Healthcare Challenges Through Collaborative Innovation


The National Center for Collaborative Healthcare Innovation (NCCHI) leads partnerships that implement cutting-edge technologies to solve healthcare’s greatest challenges for Veterans. Located in Silicon Valley, NCCHI collaborates broadly with industry partners, other government agencies, academia, and throughout VA to create far-reaching positive impact for our Veterans.

Our Work

Augmented Reality and 5G

Project Convergence brings “science fiction to life” through its cutting-edge 5G and augmented reality program.



Read More »

Fall Prevention Projects

In 2015 the cost of fatal and non-fatal falls in the US was approximately $50 billion – and since falls disproportionally impacts older adults, it is a particularly important issue for VA to address.

Read More »

COVID Operational Readiness

During the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, front-line clinical teams in many parts of the world were overwhelmed, overworked and understaffed.

Read More »

CXPRIZE Alliance to combat COVID-19

Our NCCHI partnership with the XPRIZE Foundation is bringing leading VA innovators next to partners from around the world to develop new ways of combatting COVID-19.

Read More »

AI with DeepMind

In collaboration with Google DeepMind and Google Health, we are studying the clinical applications of machine-learning. The initial work with VA data demonstrated that a Google machine learning algorithm can predict acute kidney injury up to two days before normally detected.

Read More »

Innovative Treatments for ALS and Prostate

NCCHI has partnered with the U.S Department of Energy, Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (LLNL) in the fight against ALS (Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis) and prostate cancer, two diseases.

Read More »

Augmented Reality Microscope

The Augmented Reality Microscope (ARM) is an advanced Artificial Intelligence and Augmented Reality enabled system which is currently programmed to detect prostate and breast cancer on pathology slides as a clinical decision support tool.

Read More »

Smart Cane for the Blind

Safe independent mobility is one of the biggest challenges for the visually impaired. Although technological tools are improving, the traditional ‘walking stick’ for the blind has not significantly changed since its inception – until now.
